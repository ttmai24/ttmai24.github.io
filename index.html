Reports

The Role of Location in Health Outcomes: Urban vs Rural California Census Tracts

Biostat 203A (Fall 2024) — University of California, Los Angeles

Examined urban–rural disparities in health outcomes across California census tracts, with a focus on cancer and heart disease prevalence.

Applied statistical analysis to evaluate differences in outcomes between urban and rural regions, and assessed whether disparities remained significant after adjusting for demographic and socioeconomic covariates (age, sex, poverty, education, and insurance coverage).

Methods: Regression analysis, covariate adjustment, comparative epidemiological modeling

Survival Analysis of Cardiovascular Mortality by Healthy Lifestyle Index

Biostatistics M215 Midterm Project — University of California, Los Angeles

Survival analysis examining the association between Healthy Lifestyle Index (HLIS) and time to cardiovascular death in a cohort of patients with stable coronary artery disease.

Methods: Kaplan–Meier survival analysis, Cox proportional hazards modeling, multivariable adjustment, and interaction testing.

Survival in Head and Neck Squamous Cell Carcinoma by Clinical Stage and Tumor Site

Biostatistics M215: Final Project Collaborative project with Stephanie Chu — University of California, Los Angeles

Examined overall survival patterns in patients with head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC), focusing on differences by clinical stage and primary tumor site.

Investigated how survival outcomes vary across cancer stages and anatomical tumor locations, given known heterogeneity in prognosis even within the same clinical classification.

Methods: Survival analysis, stratified comparisons by clinical stage and tumor site, epidemiological interpretation of prognostic factors

Investigating Associations Between Antidepressant Use and Demographic & Socioeconomic Factors

Biostatistics 218 (Fall 2025) — University of California, Los Angeles

Examined associations between antidepressant prescription patterns and demographic, socioeconomic, and health-related variables in a U.S. population dataset.

Focused on how social determinants of health (including race, income, education, and healthcare access) and clinical indicators (e.g., PHQ-2, K6, comorbidities) relate to antidepressant usage.

Methods: Multivariable regression modeling, exploratory data analysis, and covariate adjustment for demographic and clinical factors
